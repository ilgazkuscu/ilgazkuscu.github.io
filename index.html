CERN / ATLAS
Higgs analysis

In high-stakes science, the answer matters less than whether the pipeline can prove it earned the answer.

What I built

U.S. housing
market analysis

Housing affordability is not one market problem. It is hundreds of local systems hidden behind incompatible data.

Challenge
  • Listings, supply, prices, and local demographics arrive in different formats and tell misleading stories when read in isolation.
What I built
  • A metro-level housing dataset joining messy listing records with Census indicators, then modeled drivers of regional price formation and affordability.
Methods
  • SQL, Python, R, Census data, regularized regression, random forests, external benchmark checks.
Insight
  • Mapped 300+ U.S. metros from 100K+ listings and found that higher inventory could coincide with higher prices, challenging a simple supply-only affordability story.

04 · Experimentation & growth analytics

A/B testing

A conversion rate can move for many reasons. A decision deserves evidence that the intervention caused the lift.

Challenge
  • Separate a meaningful campaign effect from random variation, bad randomization, and misleading subgroup patterns.
What I built
  • A decision-ready evaluation of an ad-versus-PSA experiment, from randomization checks through inference, uncertainty, subgroup correction, and revenue translation.
Methods
  • Python, SciPy, two-proportion z-tests, chi-squared tests, bootstrap confidence intervals, Bonferroni-corrected subgroup analysis.
Result
  • Across 588K sessions, measured a +0.77 percentage-point conversion lift with p < 0.0001 and translated it into projected monthly contribution.

Diplomacy video
analysis

Diplomatic meetings contain consequential verbal and nonverbal cues, yet the evidence is rarely organized in a way research can revisit.

Challenge

What I built

Methods

08 · Forecasting & decision intelligence

U.S. GDP
nowcasting

Economic decisions cannot wait for the official number. They need a disciplined view while the data is still incomplete.

Challenge
  • GDP is released after the period it describes, while decision-makers receive indicators at different frequencies and with different delays.
What I built
  • A reusable pre-release nowcasting workflow that tests high-frequency indicators, residual diagnostics, and out-of-sample behavior before producing a forecast.
Methods
  • ARIMA/ARIMAX, statsmodels, pandas, NumPy, residual diagnostics, backtesting across 30 macroeconomic indicators.
Result
  • Forecast U.S. real GDP growth at 2.2% before the official release, turning delayed information into an earlier macroeconomic decision signal.

09 · ML & operational risk

U.S. accident
severity prediction

Safety data matters only when people can find the patterns early enough to make a better operating decision.

Challenge
  • National accident records are large, imbalanced, and difficult to turn into interpretable risk patterns for nontechnical users.
What I built
  • A four-class severity model and interactive Plotly Dash workflows for filtering, EDA, statistical testing, and national-scale pattern detection.
Methods
  • Python, class-balanced Gradient Boosting, scikit-learn, Plotly Dash, reproducible analysis on large transportation records.
Result
  • Reached 0.76 weighted F1 across 7.7M records, converting raw accident history into an operational-risk interface.
